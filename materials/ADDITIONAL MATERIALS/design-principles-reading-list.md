# Design principles reading list

A curated collection of essential books and videos on graphic design fundamentals.

---

## Required readings

### Foundational texts

| Book | Author | Why it matters |
|------|--------|----------------|
| **Grid Systems in Graphic Design** | Josef Müller-Brockmann | The definitive work on grid systems from legendary Swiss designer. Essential for understanding layout and structure. |
| **Interaction of Color** | Josef Albers | Life-changing insight into color perception from Bauhaus master. Still the gold standard for understanding how colors behave. |
| **Thinking with Type** | Ellen Lupton | The best typography primer. Covers alignment, spacing, hierarchy, and shaping text. Accessible and practical. |
| **The Elements of Graphic Design** | Alex W. White | Strong introduction to space, unity, page architecture, and type. Good for building foundational knowledge. |
| **Universal Principles of Design** | Lidwell, Holden & Butler | Encyclopedia-style reference covering 125 design concepts across all design disciplines. Great for dipping in and out. |

### Broader design thinking

| Book | Author | Why it matters |
|------|--------|----------------|
| **The Design of Everyday Things** | Don Norman | Classic on human-centered design. Teaches you to see how objects communicate (or fail to communicate) their use. |
| **Ways of Seeing** | John Berger | How we perceive images and visual culture. Short, provocative, and changes how you look at everything. |
| **Steal Like an Artist** | Austin Kleon | Quick read on creativity and influence. Good for understanding that all design builds on what came before. |

### History and context

| Book | Author | Why it matters |
|------|--------|----------------|
| **History of Graphic Design** | Jens Müller & Julius Wiedemann | Comprehensive visual history. Expensive but worth browsing to understand design evolution. |
| **Designing Brand Identity** | Alina Wheeler | Standard reference for branding projects. Useful process framework. |

---

## Where to start

**For readings:** Start with *Thinking with Type* (typography) and *The Design of Everyday Things* (design thinking). These two will give you the strongest foundation.

**For videos:** The Futur's typography content and Envato Tuts+ principles series are the most focused and fall within a digestible 10-30 minute range.

---

## YouTube videos (10-30 minutes)

### The Futur (Chris Do)

One of the best channels for design education. Chris Do is an Emmy-winning director and design educator.

- [The Futur YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/user/TheSkoolRocks) — Individual videos on typography, layout, and composition
- **Typography Manual** — Animated guide covering hierarchy, legibility, and balance
- **Typography Playlist** — Multiple videos on type fundamentals based on Basel School of Design principles

Key principles covered: hierarchy (primary, secondary, tertiary reads), flow, legibility, grouping, and contrast (scale, texture, color, density, negative space).

### Envato Tuts+

Well-structured tutorial videos broken into digestible modules:

- [The principles of design](https://design.tutsplus.com/articles/the-principles-of-design--cms-33962) — Covers balance, contrast, emphasis, movement, proportion, rhythm, and unity
- [Graphic design fundamentals course](https://www.classcentral.com/course/youtube-learn-the-fundamentals-of-graphic-design-with-envato-tuts-260283) — Color theory, composition rules, visual hierarchy
- Modules on design styles across decades, 65 key design terms, and typography

### Satori Graphics

Focused on core principles and design psychology:

- Videos on layout design mastery
- Breakdown of complex techniques into step-by-step tutorials
- Good for understanding the "why" behind design decisions

### Flux Academy (Ran Segall)

Project-based approach:

- "How to Choose Colors (Easy 3-Step Process)"
- "Complete Layout Guide"
- Strong on practical application

### GCFGlobal — Beginning Graphic Design

Free educational series with individual 10-15 minute videos:

- [Fundamentals of design](https://edu.gcfglobal.org/en/beginning-graphic-design/fundamentals-of-design/1/) — Line, shape, form, texture, balance
- Videos on typography, color, layout, and composition
